Innovative research shows that introducing freshwater fish to rice paddies can reduce the spread of schistosomiasis by naturally controlling the snail populations that carry the disease. At the same time, this ecological approach boosts rice yields by 25 percent, offering a powerful tool to simultaneously improve public health and local economies.
