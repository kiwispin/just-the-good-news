An exceptionally wet rainy season, bolstered by a wetter-than-normal El Niño phenomenon, is delivering widespread rainfall across Mexico from north to south. Meteorologists project that these consistent rains could successfully bring an end to the nation's challenging six-year dry spell by the end of October.
