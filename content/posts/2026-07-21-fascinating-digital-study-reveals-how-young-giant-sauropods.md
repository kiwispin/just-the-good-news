By using advanced digital modeling, researchers discovered that two elephant-sized South American dinosaurs possessed exceptionally strong bones that allowed them to easily stand upright. This clever natural design likely helped the younger, growing dinosaurs successfully forage from tall treetops and navigate their prehistoric environments.
