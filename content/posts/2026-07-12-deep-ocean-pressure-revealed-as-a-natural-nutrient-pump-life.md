Scientists have discovered that extreme pressure in the deep sea squeezes essential nutrients out of sinking organic matter, creating an unexpected and vital food source for deep-ocean microbes. This groundbreaking finding reveals a highly resilient underwater food web and provides researchers with crucial new insights into how our oceans naturally store carbon.
