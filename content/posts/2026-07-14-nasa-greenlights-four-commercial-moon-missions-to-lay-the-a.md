NASA has awarded nearly $600 million for four commercial lunar landings slated for late 2028, aiming to establish a sustainable human presence on the Moon. These missions will deploy advanced instruments to study lunar dust, map radiation, and improve navigation, paving the way for future rovers and permanent infrastructure.
