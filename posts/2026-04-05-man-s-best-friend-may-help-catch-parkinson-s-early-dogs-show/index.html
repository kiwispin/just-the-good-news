Man's Best Friend May Help Catch Parkinson's Early: Dogs Show Remarkable Ability to Detect Disease Years in Advance

Good Good Good 5 April 2026

Photo: adam hilles / Unsplash

Researchers from the Universities of Bristol and Manchester have discovered that dogs can identify Parkinson's disease with 98% accuracy, potentially years before symptoms appear in patients. This breakthrough could transform early detection and treatment approaches, offering hope to millions at risk of developing the neurodegenerative condition. The findings highlight how our canine companions continue to reveal unexpected ways they can support human health and wellbeing.
