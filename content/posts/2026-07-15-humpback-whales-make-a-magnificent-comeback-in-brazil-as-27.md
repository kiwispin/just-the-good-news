Following the end of commercial whaling, Brazil's humpback whale population has rebounded remarkably from just 2,000 individuals in 1986 to around 35,000 today. This conservation success story has sparked a thriving local whale-watching industry in regions like Guanabara Bay, bringing tourists closer to these ocean giants than ever before.
