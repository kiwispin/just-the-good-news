Following more than four decades of dedicated conservation efforts in Western Australia, the numbat population has rebounded from a low of 300 to over 3,000 individuals. This significant recovery has prompted the IUCN to officially downlist the species on its Red List, marking a major milestone for the region's biodiversity.
